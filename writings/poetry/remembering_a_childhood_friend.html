Remembering a Childhood Friend

By Chad A. Steed

May 5, 2018


I thought about you today,
As two boys played a game of football;
They played until the sunlight faded away,
Much like we did every fall.

And few are the days that come to an end,
When I'm not reminded of our childhood days:
As much time together as we did spend,
A reminder lurks in every gaze.

We ranged the piney woods, so vast,
And we pedaled our bikes on a country lane;
All too soon our adventures were in the past,
And a bond like brothers was our gain.

And when into adulthood did we advance,
We lost contact, lives diverted on different trails:
We reunited a decade later, by chance,
And spent an evening recounting our wildest tales.

Thereafter, when a memory appeared,
We sent texts and laughed at the thought;
It's funny how we each remembered,
Details that the other forgot.

And I saw you rise from a miry place,
And you unselfishly helped others too:
Chains broken, you sang of God's grace,
Using the very gifts He bestowed upon you.

Then our conversations stopped again,
I failed to reconnect, although I tried;
And a strange dread brewed within,
Until one day I got a call -- you died.

Since then, our memories playback differently,
Like an acoustic rendition of a song, minus the voice:
The new perspective makes me ponder the past deeply,
And I find it more of a treasure, and thus rejoice.

Gone a year now, oh how I miss my friend,
Yet it comforts me to know heaven was your gain:
I pray the reminiscences of our childhood never end,
They bring a smile to my face, despite the twinge of pain.
